Rules of Uno

The goal of Uno is to be the first player to get rid of all your cards.

Gameplay:

  • On your turn, play a card that matches the color or number of the top card on the discard pile.
  • If you cannot play a card, draw one from the draw pile. If the drawn card can be played, you may do so immediately; otherwise, your turn ends.
  • You cannot end the game with a special card in your hand.

Special Cards:

  • Wild Draw Four: Forces the next player to draw four cards and skip their turn. The player who played this card chooses the new color to be matched. The next player can also play a Wild Draw Four. In this case, the total number of cards to be drawn is cumulative.
  • Wild: Allows the player to choose the color that continues the play.
  • Skip: The next player loses their turn.
  • Reverse: Reverses the direction of play.
  • Draw Two: Forces the next player to draw two cards and skip their turn. The player who played this card chooses the color to be matched. The next player can also play a Draw Two. In this case, the total number of cards to be drawn is cumulative.
